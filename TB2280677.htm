Background

The Producer Price Index (PPI) is designed to measure the changes in the price of commodities charged by domestic producers and those paid by importers for importing goods into Malaysia. The first PPI was compiled for Peninsular Malaysia for reference year 1973 with 1972 as its base year. Since then, it has undergone through a few series of development which has not only significantly improve the leading economic indicators in the country. Subsequent to 1972 base years 1978, 1989, 2000, 2005 and is currently compiled using 2010 as the base year.

 

Objective

The PPI measures the average change in the prices charged by producers of goods of an industry in a reference month compared with that of the base period. Thus, it is an important macroeconomic indicator used for monitoring the price movements of local output and is often viewed as leading indicator of pressure on consumer price inflation.

 

Scope

The PPI covers the five broad sectors of economy that agriculture, forestry and fishing, mining, manufacturing and electricity & gas and water supply. The indices are published at three digit group level, 2 digit division levels and one digit category level for Malaysia. A total of 657 commodity items are used in the compilation of the local production producer price index.

PPI for local production by stage of processing is compiled by reclassifying the commodities according to the following stage of processing:

Methodology

Classification

Industry-based product classifications used are based on the Malaysia Standard Industrial Classification 2008 (M.S.I.C) and Standard International Trade Classification Rev.4. The industry breakdown of PPI for local production is classified according to the M.S.I.C 2008, which generally follows the International Standard Industrial Classification (ISIC) Rev.4, however with some modifications to suit the economic structure of Malaysia. The PPI for import is produced using the S.I.T.C developed by the United Nations which down to the three-digit level.

Data Selection

1.       Selection of Product
Number of sample commodities in the monthly price survey is relatively small compared to the population size. The selection of the products for the monthly producer price survey is based on their contributions to the total output of the industry. The selected items are representative of their group. The items included in the compilation of the index collectively accounted for more than 85 per cent of the total output of the industry selected.

2.       Selection of Establishments

The selection of establishments is based on their contributions to the total output of the selected industry. The selected establishments are representative of their products. These establishments collectively accounted for more than 85 per cent of the total output of the products selected. The lists of establishments are drawn from the Economic Census, 2010 as well as from other supplementary sources. The sample covers 1,165 establishments selected on a cut-off basis from the monthly and annual manufacturing frames.

3.       Selection of Commodities

At present, about 657 commodities are covered from 1,165 establishments for local production monthly in the compilation of PPI. These commodities are selected in view of their relative importance within the establishments. About 2,350 products had been selected for locally produced goods for the base year 2010.

Definition(s)

Local Production Price

The local production price is an output based index, which measures the change in price of commodities sold to domestic market at the point of leaving the factory gate.

Import Price Index

The import price index is an input based index which measures the change in the price of raw materials and intermediate goods imported by the local producers for the production of their final products and are valued at purchasers' prices.

Domestics Economy Price Index

The Domestics Economy Price Index is a composite index which is based on the price data derived from that of the local production and import price indices. It reflects the price movements of commodities used in the domestic economy.

 

Release Schedule

Producer Price Index is compiled and released at monthly interval. Data is compiled at monthly interval and released to the general public on the last working day of the month following the reference month.
