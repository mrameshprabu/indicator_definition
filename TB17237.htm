Indonesia: PIPU System

Source: Bank of Indonesia

PIPU - Pusat Informasi Pasar Uang (Money Market Information Center)

Definition (s)

Money Market Information System (PIPU)

Money Market Information System (PIPU) is an automatic system providing the information which covers but not limited on money market of Rupiah and Foreign Currency, as well as other information related to money market for members subscribers and Bank Indonesia it self.

Bank of Indonesia implement PIPU based on the data entry of money market (Rp and FE) supplied by PIPU member and Bank of Indonesia through electronic data processing to produce PIPU daily output. PIPU member should report the data to Bank Indonesia trough PIPU completely and correct on workdays.


Deposit Rate

Deposit is the funds in Rupiahs and foreign currencies entrusted by the public in the form of demand deposit, time deposits, certificates of deposits, saving deposits, and/or other equivalent form.

Saving deposit rate is the real deposit interest rate in market in a form of saving. It is the interest rate gives by bank to the depositor who will save the fund in saving. If banks offer different interest rate for various savings, so the reported interest rate is the lowest and highest interest rate for all kind of savings on the reporting date.

Deposit Rate: Maximum Guarantee: Bank of Indonesia

Maximum Guarantee rate is the interest rate determined by Bank of Indonesia as the maximum interest rate of the third party saving guarantee in Bank according to matured time (1, 3, 6, 12 and 24 months) and the guarantee against Interbank Money Market.

The maximum interest rate on Deposits in Rupiahs guaranteed by the Government is stipulated as the weighted average SBI discount rate for the 3 (three) months tenor formed in the latest auction, plus or minus a specified Margin.

The Maximum interest rate on Deposits in the US Dollar foreign currency guarantee by the Government is stipulated as the average rate for US Dollar time deposits at JIBOR member banks as stipulated by Bank Indonesia for a specified tenor, plus or minus a specified Margin.

The Maximum interest rate on Deposits in non-US dollar foreign currencies guaranteed by the Government is stipulated as no more than the maximum interest rate on Deposits in the US dollar currency.

The maximum interests rate on Deposits in Rupiah and foreign currencies guaranteed by the Government shall be announced by Bank Indonesia each months on a regular basis 2 (two) working days prior to the beginning of the month of the effective guarantee period and shall be effective for 1 (one) month.


Jakarta Interbank Offered Rate (Jibor)

Jakarta Interbank Offered rate is the inter bank rate for various time points offering by certain banks in Jakarta. The members of Jibor Banks are banks designated by Bank Indonesia for reference in setting the JIBOR.


Lending Rates

Inter bank Call Money is the borrowing and lending call money activities inter conventional banks in a short term either in Rupiah or foreign currency with the maximum period is 1 year.

The conventional banks doing the transaction based on Syariah principal was reported to form 102 (PUAS) and it was only allowed as the fund investor.

The Interbank Call Money Rates cover:

1. Interbank Call Money AM Session Rupiah is domestic interbank transaction using Rupiah until 12 AM local time in Jakarta.

2. Interbank Call Money PM Session Rupiah is domestic interbank transaction using Rupiah after 12 AM to 6 PM local time in Jakarta.

3. Interbank Call Money USD is Domestic interbank transaction using Foreign Currency (USD) until 6 PM local time in Jakarta.

4. Interbank Overseas Bank is Interbank transaction between bank doing its operational in Indonesia with banks doing its operational in overseas either the head office is in Indonesia or overseas. For example: Interbank between Lippo Bank Jakarta and BNI New York, this transaction only reported by Lippo Banks


Base Lending Rate

Base Lending Rate is the interest rate used as the base of commercial interest rate determination to debtor (cost of loanable fund plus margin) either for Rupiah credit or Foreign Exchange (USD).

Certificate: SBI Rate: Bank of Indonesia

Bank Indonesia Certificates hereinafter referred to as SBI, are securities denominated in the Rupiah currency issued by Bank Indonesia as short-term debt instruments (1 and 3 months).

SBI Rate is weighted average of SBI in Rupiah from the result of Bank Indonesia auction for 1 an 3 Months.


Certificate of Deposits: Bank of Indonesia

Time Deposit Rate or Certificate Deposit (Rupiah and Foreign Exchange/USD) is the real loan interest in market that is needed in order to monitor trend of loan interest rate. The reported interest rate is the highest and lowest rates from each matured time deposits of deposit rate and certificate deposits on the reporting date. This deposit rate or certificate deposit is reported according to the matured time.


Transaction Exchange Rate of Bank of Indonesia

Exchange rate is the price of a currency to other currency. Transaction exchange rate is buying or selling rate to some currencies (hard currencies) against Rupiah determined everyday by Bank of Indonesia for the purpose of Bank Indonesia's transaction with government.


TOD/TOM/SPOT Transaction

TOD/TOM/SPOT Transaction is the devisa transaction, between foreign exchange and foreign exchange or foreign exchange and Rupiah, done by bank in Indonesia with other bank or non bank either in Indonesia or overseas.

1. TOD is the devisa transaction with the same day settlement
2. TOM is the devisa transaction, the settlement is one next working day
3. SPOT is the devisa transaction, the settlement day is two next working days


SWAP RATES

SWAP is the transaction based on the contract to do the currency exchange through cash payment or term payment with periodic reselling, cash reselling or periodic with the periodic re-buying continually with the same party and at the same price which is agreed on the transaction date.

Release Schedule

Data is compiled and released every month.
