Japan: Building Construction Starts

Source: Information and Research Department, Policy Bureau, Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport

Background

From the statistics of Building Construction Starts, the number of constructed buildings and planned amount of construction expenses can be obtained monthly for the items of structures (wooden, SRC, RC, etc) and uses (office, store, factory, etc.).

Definition(s)

Type of use
a) Office
b) Store
c) Factory
d) Work room (Factories)
e) Warehouse
f) School building
g) Hospital
h) Others

Structure
a) Wooden
    The main structure (which, hereafter, will be as defined in Item 5, Article 2, Building Standard Law) is wooden
    (including wooden structure with cement rending and timber flamed structure with clay finishing).

b) Steel-frame Reinforce Concrete (SRC)
    The main structure is the unification of a steel-frame and the reinforce concrete.

c) Reinforced Concrete (RC)
    The main structure is unification of steel reinforcement within a mold and concrete poured into the mold.

d) Steel-frame structure (S)
    The main structure is steel-frame or other metal frame. (This category includes those added with steel-frame
    or those with lightweight steel-frame)

e) Concrete block (CB)
    The main structure is concrete block with steel reinforcement. (This category includes out-wall block
    structures.)

f) Others
    The main structures that do not fall into any of the above structure such as masonry, brick, plain concrete,     in plain concrete block, and so on.

Methodology

Based on stipulations in Item 1 of Article 15, Building Standard Law, when desiring to construct a building, a builder must file a report to the Prefectural Governor. The Building Starts Statistics Survey provides the data for this report.

The subject buildings of Statistics on Building Construction Starts include new buildings to be constructed all over Japan.

Scope

Since the statistics make use of reports based on the Building Standard Law, it does not include builds that were not reported or buildings covering less than 10 square meters, which are outside the scope of the statistics.
