Thailand: Laem Chabang Port

Source: Port Authority of Thailand

Background

Laem Chabang Port is Thailand's premier deep-sea commercial port, under the management of the Port Authority of Thailand (PAT), located on the Eastern shore of the upper Gulf of Thailand, approximately 110 km south of Bangkok. Laem Chabang Port is one of the most modern and advanced ports in Southeast Asia and has positioned itself as the most efficient gateway to Thailand and the greater Indochina region.

Laem Chabang Port began operations in 1991 with the goal of upgrading the basic infrastructure necessary for the economic development of Thailand and was designed as an integral part of the Eastern Seaboard project. The congested conditions at Bangkok Port, plus the port’s inability to serve large ocean-going container ships, made the Port a natural development in Thailand's economic expansion. Laem Chabang Port has grown is capacity and utilization over its decade of operation and has emerged as a key port on the international shipping network.

Laem Chabang Port covers an area of over 1,000 hectares and provides a comprehensive range of services, including cargo handling, warehousing, cargo distribution under railway and highway networks system leading to all parts of Thailand. Port services include pilotage and towage, supply of fresh water, power supply, telecommunications, sewage treatment, garbage collection and disposal. In addition to above services, ship repair and maintenance services provided by floating dock yard with capacity approximately 140,000 DWT under the management of private sector, are also available at the northern part of the Port. The Port also provides round-the-clock security, environmental control and fire fighting system.

Laem Chabang Port presently operates 11 terminals to accommodate various types of vessels including container ships, bulk carriers, pure car carriers and passenger liners. Among the terminals, five are container terminals (B1 to B5), three are coastal terminals (A1) including a bulk terminal (A4) and two general cargo terminals (A1 and A5). The remaining terminals have yet to be completed, and are in various stages of design and construction (A0, A2 and A3) adding to the future capacity of the Port. More than 80 percent of sea borne cargo handled by Laem Chabang Port is containerized.

Laem Chabang Port stands as the hub of key infrastructure upgrades designed to capitalize on Thailand's economic strengths and position as Southeast Asia's gateway to Indochina. Already linked efficiently by land, rail and sea within Thailand, upgraded facilities at the Utapao airport will facilitate cargo links to Laem Chabang Port by air.
