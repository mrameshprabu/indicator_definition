Indonesia: Gross Domestic Product

Source: Indonesia Statistic Bureau (BPS)

Background

Indonesia's GDP statistics are published in both constant prices and at current prices. In the presentation of GDP at current prices, all aggregate data are valued based on their current prices. In the presentation of GDP at constant prices, all aggregate data are valued at their base year prices. Therefore, for the constant price GDP, the year to year movements of the aggregate data reflect changes In real production rather than changes in prices.
The GDP at current prices has advantages for showing economic structures, and the GDP at constant prices for knowing economic progress.

 

Definition(s)

GDP by Expenditure
GDP by Expenditure is the total value or components of final demand, covering consumption of households and private non profit institutions, government consumption, gross fixed capital formation, changes in stock, and net exports (exports deducted by imports) for the period. Indonesia's GDP by Expenditure is classified into the following components:

- Expenditures of Household and nonprofit institution;
- Government consumption;
- Gross domestic fixed capital formations;
- Change in stocks; and
- Net export (refers to export minus import).

GDP by Sector
This is the total value of final goods and services produced by all production units during the period. Indonesia's GDP by sector is grouped into the following:

- Agriculture; Livestock, Forestry and Fishery;
- Mining and Quarrying;
- Manufacturing;
- Electricity, Gas and Water Supply;
- Construction;
- Trade, Hotel and Restaurant
- Transport and Communication;
- Bank, Rental and Business Services; and
- Services, including services provided by government.

Each sector is further divided into sub-sectors.

Gross National Product (GNP)
Gross National Product (GNP) is GDP plus net Income from abroad. The net Income covers Income from abroad of production factors owned by residents deducted by income from Indonesia production factors owned by non residents.

National Income
National Income is defined as GNP deducted by net indirect tax and depreciation. Also known as Net National Product at factor cost.

Net National Product
Net National product is defined as GNP minus depreciation of capital goods used in production process for a year.

Net National Product at Factor Cost
Net National Product at Factor Cost is defined as the GNP at current prices minus net indirect taxes. The term net is the indirect taxes minus government subsidies.

Per Capita Figures
Per Capita Figures are obtained by dividing the indicators above by the total population at mid-year.

 

Notes and Remarks

Since 1994, GDP at constant prices has been presented based on 1993 prices, grouped into 9 sectors
