Thailand: Direct Investment Abroad

Source: Bank of Thailand

Background

Direct investment abroad (or outward Thai Direct Investment) reflects the lasting interests of residents of an economy in a nonresident entity. A direct investor may invest in the forms of equity capital, lending to affiliates, or reinvested earnings.

� Investment in equity where the direct investors owns 10 percent or more of the ordinary shares or voting power for an incorporated enterprise or the equivalent for an unincorporated enterprise.

� Loans from affiliated refers to borrowing and lending of funds between direct investors and subsidiaries, branches and associates. Excluded inter-office loans to/from finance companies, which are included as "other investment" instead.

� Reinvested earnings is defined as investment earnings not distributed as dividends nor remitted to direct investors.

 

Notes and Remarks

On the contrary, positive figures means the increases in investment during the period are lower than the decreases.

Release Schedule

The Thai direct investment abroad is compiled and released at monthly interval. The statistic is announced through press release to the public and is also posted on the Bank of Thailand website on the month-end date.
