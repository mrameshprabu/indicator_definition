Background

The Consumer price index (CPI) is calculated by comprehensively measuring the price fluctuation of goods and services purchased by households nationwide in the time series. It reflects changes in the cost of purchasing goods and services in a fixed �consumption patterns�.

Scope

As the CPI is designed to measure changes in prices of goods and services that affect the household life as consumers, the scope of items used for calculation is limited to household consumption expenditure.

However, among the consumption expenditures, religious contributions, donations, money gifts, other obligation fees and remittances are excluded from the scope of the CPI because the markets do not generally exist for such items, the relationship between payment and counter value is not clear or they involve income transfer to other households.

Besides, the CPI does not cover non-consumption expenditures such as income taxes and social insurance premiums and disbursements other than expenditures such as savings, security purchases, and property purchases such as land and houses. It is notably that the indirect taxes such as consumption taxes that are imposed upon purchasing of goods and services are included in scope of CPI.

As regards the housing cost of owner-occupied dwellings, the rental equivalence approach (imputed rent) is applied.

Base period and reference period for weights
Both the base period and the reference period for weights are the calendar year 2015.

Items to be price
There are a total of 585 items used for the computation of the index, which consisting of 584 items and 1 item of imputed rent for owner-occupied dwellings. Items are selected in consideration of the relative importance of each item to the total living expenditures, representativeness of price movement of all the goods and services purchased by households.

Computation of Index
Procedure of the computation:
The sub-indices of the most detailed groups are firstly calculated by aggregating item indices with weights assigned to each item. The sub-indices of the minor groups are then calculated by aggregating the sub-indices of the most detailed groups with weights assigned to each group. The sub-indices for middle groups, ten major groups and the general index are calculated by the same procedure.

As for the general index for the whole country, item indices for each municipality are calculated and then the average item indices for the whole country are calculated as the weighted arithmetic means using weights assigned to each municipality. Then the sub-indices for middle groups and tem major groups and the general index are calculated with weights of the whole country. Indices for regions are also calculated in the same manner.

Price data
1. In principle, prices of the items are obtained by the Trend Survey in the Retail Price Survey
2. For the three items "Desktop PCs", "Notebook PCs", and "Cameras", the prices of all products sold at major electric appliances shops nationwide are obtained through the scanner data from the Point of Sale system. Hedonic method is applied to compile price indices

Weights


1. The weights used for calculating the index by fixed-base method are computed based on the average monthly expenditures by item per household in 2015, which derived from the result of Family Income and Expenditure Survey 2015.
2. However, due to large fluctuation in the monthly purchases quantities of fresh food (fresh fish & seafood, fresh vegetables and fresh fruits), the monthly weights of fresh food are calculated using monthly purchase quantities 2014 and 2015 in addition to result of Family Income and Expenditure Survey 2015.
3. The weights used for �Imputed Rent� are computed based on result from National Survey of Family Income Expenditure 2014.
4. The weights used for calculating the index by chain-linking method are updated every year using the results of Family Income and Expenditure in the previous year.
