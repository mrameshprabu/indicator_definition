Japan: Trade Statistics

Source: The Summary Report on Trade of Japan, Japan Tariff Association

Background

Statistic timing

a)

Exports

 

Entry is made to a month to which belongs a date when any ship or aircraft loading the goods concerned leaves a Japanese port.

 

 

b)

Imports

 

Entry is made to a month to which a date of an import permit belongs.

Scope

Source of Data
The monthly return of Trade Statistics is compiled on the basis of information contained in such declarations, as export declarations, import declarations, re-shipment declarations, etc, which were submitted to the customs for clearance, and declarations inward / outward of vessel, etc.

Coverage
The export and import statistics shown in the monthly return do not include the following goods:

 1) Goods of small amount of values of less than 200,000 "yen",
 2) Samples,
 3) Goods smuggled,
 4) Gifts,
 5) Ship's and aircraft's stores,
 6) Personal effects of travelers,
 7) Goods of performance of traveling entertainers,
 8) Goods belonging to US and UN Forces,
 9) Exhibits to the Fair,
10) Containers of repeated use.

Classification of goods
Goods are classified according to "The Basic Classification Index for The Summary Report on Trade of Japan". This classification is based upon "Commodity Classification for Foreign Trade Statistics" based upon HS, SITC and other classification.

Regional coverage of "Japan" as referred to in this Return and classification of goods by country (Place)
a) Through this return, the term "Japan" means Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu and Hokkaido as well as the islands     belonging to those areas.
b) In principal, exports are classified by country (place) of destination, whereas import by Country (place) of     origin.

Notes and Remarks

Quantity and value
a) The unit of quantity is indicated on the basis of the one provided in "Commodity Classification for Foreign Trade Statistics.
b) The value of exports is based upon the F.O.B. value, where as the value of imports upon the C.I.F. value.

Coverage of EU

Coverage of trade statistics between Japan and EU varies based on the chronology of EU expansion.

January 1988 - December 1994
Number of EU member countries: 12
Countries: Denmark, United Kingdom, Ireland, Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, France, Germany, Portugal, Spain, Italy and Greece

January 1995 - April 2004
Number of EU member countries: 15
Countries: Denmark, United Kingdom, Ireland, Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, France, Germany, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Greece, Sweden, Finland and Austria

May 2004 - December 2006
Number of EU member countries: 25
Countries: Denmark, United Kingdom, Ireland, Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, France, Germany, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Greece, Sweden, Finland, Austria, Malta, Poland, Hungary, Cyprus, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Slovenia, Czech Republic and Slovakia

January 2007 onwards
Number of EU member countries: 27
Countries: Denmark, United Kingdom, Ireland, Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, France, Germany, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Greece, Sweden, Finland, Austria, Malta, Poland, Hungary, Cyprus, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Slovenia, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Romania and Bulgaria

Commodity Classification Based on HS Classification

Data of export and import by commodity from January 2007 are compiled according to HS classification (2007 version). Prior to January 2007, corresponding data are based on HS classification (2002 version).

Release Schedule

Provisional trade statistics and detailed report of exports are released a month after the applicable month separately and the detailed report of imports is released 2 months after the applicable month.
