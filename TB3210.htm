India: Foreign Exchange Rates: Reserve Bank of India

 

Source: Reserve Bank of India

Methodology

The Reserve Bank of India compiles and publishes reference rates for Spot USD/INR and Spot EUR/INR on a daily basis. The rates are derived by averaging the mean of the bid/offer rates polled from selected banks around 12 noon every weekday (excluding Saturdays, Sundays and Bank Holidays in Mumbai).� The contributing banks are selected on the basis of their standing, market share in the domestic foreign exchange market and representative character.

Effective from 1 July 2011, Reserve Bank of India has decided to revise the existing methodology to ensure that the reference rate is a true reflection of market activity. The Foreign Exchange Rates will be polled from the selected list of contributing banks at a randomly chosen five minute window between 11.45 a.m. and 12.15 p.m. every weekday (excluding Saturdays, Sundays and Bank Holidays in Mumbai). The reference rate of spot US Dollar and Euro against Rupee shall be disseminated up to the fourth decimal place.�

Effective from 1 September 2014, Reserve Bank of India has further revised the previous methodology. The rate for spot US Dollar against Indian Rupee will be polled from the selected list of contributing banks at a randomly chosen five minute window between 11.30 a.m. and 12.30 p.m. every weekday (excluding Saturdays, Sundays and Bank Holidays in Mumbai). Besides, the other three rates (EUR/INR, GBP/INR and JPY/INR) would be computed by crossing the USD/INR Reference Rate with the prevailing EUR/USD, GBP/USD and USD/JPY rates.

 

Release Schedule
