Philippines: Foreign Direct Investment

Source: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas

 

Background

Foreign Direct Investment Statistics includes non-resident investments in the Philippines, specifically, equity capital (other than reinvestment of earnings), reinvestment of earnings and transactions in debt instruments or intercompany borrowings and �lending.

 

Scope

Based on BPM6, direct investment is a category of cross border investment associated with a resident in one economy having control or a significant degree of influence on the management of an enterprise that is a resident in another economy. Operationally, direct investment in an enterprise is indicated by ownership of at least 10 percent of equity shares. Less than 10 percent ownership is considered as portfolio investments.

 

 

Methodology

Classification

Based on BPM6, direct investments can be in the form of equity capital, reinvestment of earnings and debt instruments. Net equity capital is broken down by industry group based on the 2009 Philippine Standard Industrial Classification (PSIC). Prior to the use of the 2009 PSIC, the 2004 PSIC is being used. Net equity capital is also broken down by country of origin. No industry and no country breakdown are available for reinvestment of earnings and debt instruments in the absence of information.

Conformity with standards

The compilation of the net incurrence of liabilities/FDI statistics, including data sources and methodologies used, conforms to the internationally accepted standards and guidelines set forth in the International Monetary Fund�s (IMF) Balance of Payments Manual, 6th Edition (BPM6) and the standards prescribed in the IMF�s Special Data Dissemination Standard. Equity capital is broken down by industry group based on the standard industrial classification.
