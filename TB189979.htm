Indonesia: Wholesale Price Index: 2010=100

Source: Central Bureau of Statistics (BPS)


Background

The Wholesale Price Index (WPI) is an index to measure between times the average price changes of goods at the level of Whole sale or prices at big sales.

Now, WPI has used a new base year namely 2010 (2010=100). WPI with 2010 base year begin to be calculated since Dec 2012. And after that time, we don't calculated WPI with base year 2005 anymore.


Objective

WPI is one of the indicators to follow the growth of the economy in general, and as material in analyzing the market and monetary conditions.

The WPI is presented in a general index and groups of commodity.


Scope

The WPI is disaggregated into five commodity groups: Agriculture, Manufacturing, Mining and Quarrying, Imports and Exports, where each sector consists of sub-commodity groups. Total number of sub-commodities is 327.

WPI data are collected from 33 provincial capital cities and 126 other potential cities/towns, and are assumed to have major companies, which trades varied goods.


Methodology

The Wholesale Price Index for construction materials is based on price changes of 26 groups of selected goods.

Respondents are selected from companies that are considered representative of trading commodities, so that all commodities covered could represent the Wholesale Price for each province.

The data is collected directly from respondents every month, through direct interview.


Release Schedule

Data is released at 2nd week of the month.

Date: February 2013
