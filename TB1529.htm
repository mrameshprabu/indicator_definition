Japan: Population Estimation

Source: Outline of the estimation, Statistics Bureau & Statistics Center, Ministry of Public Management, Home Affairs, Posts and Telecommunication

Methodology

"Current population estimates" are computed based on the Population Census, carried once every 5 years. The formula for estimating the population as of the 1st of the month is as follows:

   Estimated Population of the stated month

 

= Population of the previous month + Natural Change of the previous month + Migration change of the previous month + Net increase by change of Nationality

 

Where

 

    Natural Change

 

=  Live Births � Deaths

 

and

 

    Migrational Change

 

= Entries - Exits

 

 

 

 


�Natural Change of population" is based on the preliminary results of the Vital Statistics by the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare. Figures for the 3 and 4 months before the current month are based on the prompt report of the Vital Statistics. As new figures are not available for the latest 2 months, the data of the previous year are used instead.

 

�Migration Change of population" is based on the Statistics on Legal Migrants compiled by the Ministry of Justice. Foreigner migrants whose period of intended stay in Japan within 90 days are excluded. All Japanese migrants are included regardless of the period of their stay in foreign countries because of the absence of data. As new figures are not available for the latest 2 months, the data of the previous year are used instead.

The population estimates as of the first day of each month are calculated using the recent census population, and so each census population would be used to estimate population for the next 5 years. However, estimated population based on Census Population 5 years ago is not necessarily equal to the population count in new population Census. Therefore, when the new Census data is available, intercensal adjustment will be carried out to retroactively revise the population data during intercensal periods.                

The latest intercensal adjustment is applied to population data from November 2005 to Sep 2010, previously estimated using 2005 Census Population data, when 2010 Census Population data becomes available.
