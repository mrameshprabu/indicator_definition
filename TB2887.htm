Background

The BCI is an index designed to measure the average rate of change in prices for 15 selected building materials utilized in 8 building categories for 6 regions in Peninsular Malaysia. Starting from January 2013 publication, the series is based on the revised base year which has been changed from (July 2008=100) to (July 2012=100) as well as the selection of new building material specifications and weightage updated by JKR.

 

Scope

Unit Price Index for Bricks & Wall, Glass, Aggregates, Ceiling Materials, Roofing Materials, Timber, Plywood, Sanitary Fittings, Floor & Wall Tiles, Plumbing Materials, Steel & Metal Sections, Sand and Paints cover all 6 region of Peninsular Malaysia.

6 region of Peninsular Malaysia

Region A = Pulau Pinang, Kedah & Perlis

Region B = Perak

Region C = Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur, Melaka, Negeri Sembilan & Selangor

Region D = Johor

Region E = Pahang

Region F = Kelantan & Terengganu

Unit Price Index for U type Sheet Piles, Square Hollow Sections, Rectangular Hollow Sections, Universal Beams, Rolled Steel Joists, Equal Angles, Unequal Angles, �U� Channels and Lipped Channels cover Selangor and Kuala Lumpur Federal Territory.

Approximately 3200 price quotations are collected monthly from 545 outlets for 164 selected building material specifications.

 

Methodology

BCI is based on the Laspeyres formula. Movements of the BCI from one month to another are expressed as percentage changes rather than changes in index points because �index point� changes are affected by the level of the index in relation to its base period while percentage changes are not.

Background

The Building Materials Cost Index (BCI) is used in Special Provision to the Conditions of Contract (Variation of Price) in government building contracts only. The specifications and weightage for 14 selected building materials in 8 building categories are provided by Public Works Department (JKR). Since January 2009 publication, the series is based on the revised base year which has been change from July 2002=100 to July 2008= 100 as well as the selection of mew building materials specifications and weightage updated by JKR. Approximately 2300 price quotations are collected monthly from 440 outlets for 158 selected materials specifications.

Starting from January 2013 publication, BCI data with base Jul2008=100 has been discontinued. A new series according to new building materials specifications and weightage updated by JKR with base July 2012=100 have been compiled.

 

Objective

The BCI is an index designed to measure the average rate of change in prices for 14 selected building materials utilized in 8 building categories for 6 regions in Peninsular Malaysia.

 

Scope

Category of Buildings

Single- Storey Building

2- 4 Storey Buildings (Flat Roof)

5 Storey and Above Buildings (For Accommodation)

5 Storey and Above Buildings (For Office)

Timber Buildings

Timber Piling

Region D = Johor

Region E = Pahang

Region F = Kelantan & Terengganu

 

Methodology

The BCI is based on Laspeyres formula. Following the liberalization of steel bars prices announced by the government on 12 May 2008, steel bars has been taken out from the calculation of BCI from May 2008. With the exclusion of steel bars, the calculation of the index is based on 14 materials instead of 15 materials. While, the cement price used in the calculation of the BCI is obtained from Construction Development Board Malaysia (CIDB).

Movements of the BCI from one month to another are expressed as percentage changes rather than changes in index points because "index points" changes are affected by the level of the index in relation to its base period while percentage changes are not.

Building Material Cost Index: Jul2002=100

Source: Special Release for Building and Structural Works 2, Department of Statistics, Malaysia

Background

The Building Material Cost Index (BCI) is a special purpose index, which is based on the Laspeyres formula. It is designed to measure the average rate of change in prices of selected building materials utilized in eight categories of building, in six regions of Peninsular Malaysia. The BCI measures price changes from a designated period, July 2002=100. The BCI is relevant only for application of the Price Variation Clause in government building contracts.

Starting from January 2009 publication, BCI data with base Jul2002=100 has been discontinued. A new series according to new building materials specifications and weightage updated by JKR with base July 2008=100 have been compiled.

 

Scope

8 categories of building

i. Single Storey

ii. Single Storey

iii. 2-4 Storey (Pitched Roof)

iv. 5 Storey & above (Building for Accommodation)

v. 5 Storey & above (Building for Office)

vi. Timber Buildings

vii. Timber Piling

viii. Reinforced Concrete Piling

 

6 region of Peninsular Malaysia

Region A = Pulau Pinang, Kedah & Perlis

Region B = Perak

Region C = Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur, Melaka, Negeri Sembilan & Selangor

Region D = Johor

Region E = Pahang

Region F = Kelantan & Terengganu

 

Methodology

The specifications and weightage pattern of selected building materials by category of building are determined by Jabatan Kerja Raya. Approximately 4300 price quotations are collected monthly from about 760 outlets for 130 selected building material items.
Movements of the Building Material Cost Index from one month to another are expressed as percentage changes rather than changes in index points because, 'index point' changes are affected by the level of the index in relation to the base period while percentage changes are not.

 

Release Schedule

BCI (2002July=100) has been rebased. It used to release at monthly interval.
