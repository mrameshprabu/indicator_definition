Thailand: Private Investment Index 2000=100

Source: Bank of Thailand

Methodology

Private Investment Index (PII) is a composite index representing private investment conditions. The computing method of index is the Foundation for International Business and Economic Research (FIBER). PII is constructed from 5 components, are as follow:

1) Construction area permitted in municipal zone
2) Domestic cement sales
3) Import of capital goods at constant price
4) Domestic machinery sales at constant price
5) Commercial car sales

Each component was seasonally adjusted and 3 month moving average except construction area which was 12 month moving average to reflect the work in progress in construction. Base year is year 2000. Consequently, the index has a highly correlation with total private investment with low Root Mean Square Percent Error (RMSPE) and Their coefficient.

 

Release Schedule

The private investment index is compiled and released at monthly interval. The statistics are announced through press release to the public and are also posted on the Bank of Thailand website on the month-end date.
