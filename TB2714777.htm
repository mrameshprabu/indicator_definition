Background

 

The Wholesale Price Index is the most widely used inflation indicator in India. This is published by the Office of Economic Adviser, Ministry of Commerce and Industry. The current series of Wholesale Price Index has 2011-12 as the base year and 697 commodities (117 Primary Articles, 16 articles under the Fuel and Power and 564 articles under the Manufactured Products).

The Wholesale Price Index is provided in monthly frequency and includes an aggregated index and its constituent components.

 

The monthly index of wholesale prices at the time of its initial compilation and release is provisional because it does not take into account some of the price quotations that are received belatedly.ï¿½ The final index is made after a period of two months by which time almost all the required price quotations are expected to be available.

 

In order to remove impact of fiscal policy in the current series of Wholesale Price Index, prices used for compilation do not include indirect tax. This will make the Wholesale Price Index conceptually closer to Producer Price Index.

 

The Wholesale Food Price Index introduced to capture the rate of inflation in food items by compiling the Food Articles under Primary Articles and Food Products under Manufactured Products. Similar to Consumer Price Index, this would help monitor the price situation of food items.

 

 

Objective

 

The Wholesale Price Index is important, as it helps in understanding the movement of prices relating to bulk transactions of purchases, usually for further sale. Conventionally, the wholesale price index has been used to measure the variations in exchange value or purchasing power of money. It has also been utilized for other purposes such as forecasting changes in business conditions, indicating supply-demand relationships, deflating aggregates such as national income, etc. The index numbers are available at the sector and sub-sector levels as well as at the overall level of the economy.

 

 

Methodology

 

The WPI is calculated on the principle of weighted arithmetic mean, according to the Laspeyres formula, which has a fixed base-year weighing diagram operative through the entire life span of the series.
