Philippines: Gross Domestic Product by Expenditure

Source: Philippine Statistics Authority

 

Background

 

The Gross Domestic Product is one of the major macroeconomic statistics compiled and disseminated by the Philippine Statistics Authority, to provide information about the performance of the Philippine economy as basis for economic analysis and policy formulation.

 

 

Methodology

 

The current Philippine System of National Accounts (PSNA) basically follows the 1968 United Nations System of National Accounts (SNA), but to a limited scale incorporates the recommendations of the 2008 SNA. The 2008 SNA did not change or alter the theoretical framework of the 1968 SNA. Instead it modified the sectoring of the system, elaborated and expanded some categories and introduced new concepts that reflect contemporary views on the economy and current financial practices. The PSNA compiles GDP through the production and expenditure approach.

 

 

Production Approach
Production Approach refers to the aggregate of the gross value added (GVA) of all resident producer units, adjusted for import duties and non-deductible value added tax (VAT) or similar taxes on products depending upon the valuation of outputs, inputs and imports. Basically, the approach can be simplified as - Gross Output less Intermediate Inputs equals GVA.

 

Expenditure Approach
Expenditure Approach refers to the sum of the final consumption of goods and services in the economy. GDP by expenditure is computed as - Personal Consumption Expenditure plus Government Consumption Expenditures plus Gross Domestic Capital Formation plus Exports less Imports.

Definition(s)

 

Gross Domestic Product
Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is the value of all goods and services produced domestically; the sum of gross value added of all resident institutional units engaged in production (plus any taxes, and minus any subsidies, on products not included in the values of their outputs)

 

Gross National Product
Gross National Product (GNP) is the GDP adjusted with the net factor income from the rest of the world. It refers to the aggregate earnings of the factors of production (nationals) plus indirect taxes (net) and capital consumption allowance.

 

Personal Consumption Expenditure

Personal Consumption Expenditure represents a record of the value of final expenditure by households and private non-profit institutions on current goods and services less their net sales of similar goods and services plus the value of gifts in kind received from the rest of the world.

 

Government Consumption Expenditure

Government Consumption Expenditures refer to government expenditures on goods and services including compensation of employees and its net purchases of goods and services less sales from said goods and services.

 

Gross Domestic Capital Formation

Gross Domestic Capital Formation is the part of a country's current output and imports which is not consumed or exported during the accounting period but is set aside as an addition to its stock of capital goods. It is composed of gross additions to fixed assets and changes in stocks.�������

 

Fixed Capital Formation

Fixed Capital Formation is the value of the purchases and own account construction of fixed assets by enterprises, households, private non-profit institutions and general government.

 

Changes in Stocks

Changes in Stocks refer to the value of the physical changes in raw materials, works-in-progress (other than works-in-progress of the building and heavy construction industries which are included in the fixed capital formation account) and finished goods held by an enterprises and in government stockpiles.

 

Net Factor Income from Abroad

Net Factor Income from Abroad (NFIA) consists of the net income receipts from the rest of the world such as (1) investments incomes including interest, dividends, and branch profits; (2) earnings of residents working abroad; and (3) other factor incomes of normal residents.

 

Statistical Discrepancy

Statistical Discrepancy is the difference between the estimates of total national output from the income side of the account, on the one hand, and from the expenditure side on the other.

 

Release Schedule

 

Data is compiled and released every three months.
